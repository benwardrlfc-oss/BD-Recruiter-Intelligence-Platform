Implement the following enhancements to improve usability, personalisation, navigation, and intelligence quality.

The goal is to make the platform feel like a personal BD intelligence assistant for executive search, where every signal, company, and investor is interconnected and actionable.

The system must remain a market intelligence and BD action engine, not a CRM or candidate database.

1. Universal Linking Between Entities (Critical System Behaviour)

The platform currently shows companies, venture firms, signals, and roles as separate items. This needs to change.

Every entity should be clickable and interconnected, creating a navigable intelligence graph.

Required linking behaviour

Users should be able to navigate seamlessly between:

Venture firms

Portfolio companies

Companies

Hiring signals

Live roles

Watch list items

Example flow

User clicks:

Venture Intel → Venture Firm → Portfolio Company → Company Profile → Hiring Signals → BD Script

Implementation rules

Every card representing:

venture firm

company

portfolio company

hiring signal

role

watchlist company

must be clickable and open the relevant detail view.

Example:

Venture Intel page

ARCH Venture Partners → click → opens VC profile

Portfolio companies list:

BioNova Therapeutics → click → opens company profile

Company profile includes:

website link

hiring signals

timeline

related investors

Add Website Links

Every company and venture firm should include a clickable website link.

Examples:

Company profile

Website: www.bionovatherapeutics.com

Investor: ARCH Venture Partners
Sector: Oncology Biotech

Clicking the website should open it in a new browser tab.

2. First-Time Onboarding Flow

Add a guided onboarding wizard that runs when a new user logs into the platform for the first time.

This should personalise the system immediately.

Step 1 – User Details

Collect:

Name
Title
Company

Example

Ben Ward
Head of Life Sciences
CSG Talent

Store this information and use the user’s name throughout the interface.

Step 2 – Define Market Scope

Allow the user to define the market they want the intelligence engine to analyse.

Fields:

Sector
Subsector / therapeutic area
Geography
Company stage

Example:

Sector: Biotech
Subsector: Oncology
Geography: USA
Stage: Seed – Series B

This should drive the entire platform.

Step 3 – Target Companies

Ask the user to add 3–5 companies they are already tracking.

Example:

Tempus
Natera
Guardant Health

These automatically populate the Watch List.

Step 4 – Upload Example CV

Allow the user to upload a sample CV.

Purpose:

Calibrate the Candidate Matcher so the system understands the type of leadership profile the recruiter typically works with.

This CV is only used for analysis and calibration, not stored as a database record.

Step 5 – Weekly Email Preferences

Allow users to choose:

Daily briefing email
Weekly market summary
Signal alerts

This completes onboarding.

3. Daily Briefing Panel

Add a Daily Briefing section at the top of the dashboard.

This should appear every time the user logs in.

The tone should feel like a personal intelligence report written specifically for the user.

Content of Daily Briefing

Personal greeting

Example

Good morning Ben.

AI market summary

One paragraph explaining what happened in the user’s market in the last 24 hours.

Example

Three oncology biotech companies announced funding rounds in Boston, signalling likely leadership expansion across discovery and clinical teams.

Top 3 hiring signals today

Example

BioNova Therapeutics – Series B – likely CSO hire
Radiant Bio – Platform expansion – Head of Biology
NeuroAxis – Clinical trial start – VP Clinical

New funding events

Example

Atlas Oncology – Series A – $42M

Recommended BD action

Example

Reach out to BioNova Therapeutics leadership regarding upcoming CSO search.

4. Company Watch List

Add a Watch List feature.

Users can pin companies they are actively monitoring.

Watch List behaviour

Users can add companies from:

Company pages
Hiring signals
Venture portfolio lists

Each watched company should have its own signal timeline.

Timeline includes:

Funding events
Job postings
Leadership changes
Pipeline updates

Example timeline

January – Series A funding
February – VP Biology hire
March – Platform expansion

Users should receive a notification when a watched company triggers a new signal.

The Watch List should be accessible from:

Sidebar navigation
Company cards across the platform

Each Signal Strength score must include an explanation of how the score was calculated.

Add either:

Tooltip
Expandable section

Example

Signal Strength: 94

Confidence Breakdown

Series B funding detected – 40 points
Leadership gap identified – 30 points
Pipeline expansion announced – 24 points

Total score: 94

This builds trust in the AI intelligence.

6. BD Scripts Generator Enhancement

Improve the BD Scripts section.

When a user selects a hiring signal the system should generate a personalised outreach script referencing that specific signal.

Script inputs

Company name
Signal type
Likely hire
Market context

Script outputs

Cold Email

LinkedIn Message

Phone Call Opener

Example

Cold Email

Subject: Quick thought on your Series B expansion

Hi [Name],

I saw BioNova Therapeutics recently closed its Series B round. At this stage many oncology companies begin strengthening their discovery leadership, particularly around CSO level.

I’ve supported similar teams through leadership builds following funding rounds and would be happy to share insight on the market.

Would it make sense to connect briefly?

Best
[Name]

Scripts must feel specific and researched, not generic templates.

7. LinkedIn Content Studio Enhancement

Each day the system should generate three content options based on market activity.

Option 1 – Market news overview

Short summary of the day’s key signals and funding events.

Option 2 – Thought leadership

Insight about a trend detected in the user’s market.

Option 3 – Conversation starter

Question or discussion prompt based on current activity.

All posts should be ready to copy and paste to LinkedIn.

Add a Post History section so users can see previously generated posts.

8. Personalisation Engine

The platform should learn from user behaviour.

Track:

Signals clicked
Companies watched
Scripts generated
Content created

Use this data to prioritise relevant signals.

Add a dashboard section:

Your Market Pulse

This dynamically surfaces the signals and companies most relevant to the user.

9. Mobile Responsiveness

The entire platform must be fully responsive.

Recruiters should be able to check intelligence on mobile.

Ensure:

Dashboard works on mobile
Signal cards adapt to smaller screens
Venture intelligence pages remain readable
Watch list works on mobile

Add a simplified mobile Daily Briefing view showing:

Greeting
Top signals
Recommended BD action

10. Floating Quick Actions Bar

Add a floating Quick Actions bar visible from every page.

Actions should include:

Scan Market Signals
Upload CV
Add Company to Watch List
Create LinkedIn Content
View BD Pipeline

This allows recruiters to take action without navigating back to the dashboard.

11. Overall Platform Experience

The platform should feel like a personal intelligence assistant built specifically for the recruiter using it.

Key design principles:

Personalised experience using the user’s name

Example

Good morning Ben.

Clean dark theme

Clear visual hierarchy

Signals and actions should always be the most prominent elements.

Every piece of intelligence should relate to the user’s defined Market Scope.

The user should always know:

What is happening in their market
Which companies are likely hiring
What BD action they should take next

Final Objective

The platform should function as a BD Intelligence Terminal for Executive Search.

Core system loop:

Scan market activity
Detect hiring signals
Explain the trigger
Predict leadership hiring
Recommend BD actions
Enable outreach

Every entity in the system must be interconnected, clickable, and navigable, allowing the user to move fluidly from venture firms to companies to signals to BD actions.
