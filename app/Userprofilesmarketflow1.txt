The Market Settings flow and broader product architecture need to be redesigned so the platform can scale across industries without breaking.

Do not treat Market Settings as just a filter form. Treat it as the core market configuration engine that produces one structured market profile used across the entire platform.

Implement the following structural principles:

1. One central market profile

The setup flow must generate a canonical market profile object that defines:

industry

subsector

niche

functional focus

seniority

company types

company stage model

geography

ownership / capital model

signal preferences

commercial model

Every other module must read from this same market profile.

2. Dynamic taxonomy by industry

Industry selection must dynamically control:

subsectors

niche options

function libraries

relevant signal types

terminology

stage models where applicable

Different industries must not share the same static options.

3. Configurable signal model

Build a signal taxonomy that supports multiple signal families such as:

capital / ownership

growth / commercial

talent / hiring

market / product

regulatory / milestone

Each market profile should determine which signal families matter most and how they should be weighted.

4. Dynamic function libraries

Functional focus options must change based on industry.
Life sciences, legal, construction, tech, finance, and other industries must all surface relevant functions.

5. Ownership / capital flexibility

Do not assume every market is VC-backed.
Support:

VC-backed

PE-backed

public company

private company

family-owned

government

non-profit

consultancy

agency

The current Venture Intelligence concept must evolve into a broader ownership / capital model where relevant.

6. Dynamic scoring model

Signal scoring and hiring inference must not be universal.
Different industries and commercial models must have different weighting logic.

7. Guided setup flow

Redesign the settings experience as a step-based, guided flow:

define market with AI builder or template

name profile

select industry

dynamically choose subsector

dynamically choose functions

choose seniority

choose company type / ownership

choose stage model

choose geography

choose signal preferences

Each step should influence the next.

8. Summary / confirmation panel

Show a clear market profile summary as the user builds it so they can confirm the platform has understood their market.

9. Versionable templates

Templates such as Life Sciences Recruiter, Tech Recruiter, Finance Recruiter, etc. should behave like structured configurations, not just visual shortcuts. They should set defaults for:

taxonomy

signals

scoring

modules

ownership model

10. Niche market fallback

If a user enters a niche that is not in the taxonomy, store it as a custom specialty while still mapping the broader market to the structured framework.

11. Universal vs industry-specific separation

Keep universal platform logic separate from industry-specific logic so the product can scale.

12. Long-term objective

The result should be a stable, scalable market configuration engine that allows every other part of the platform to stay accurate, dynamic, and personalised without becoming brittle.
