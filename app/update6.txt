Update the product so the entire app is personalised and dynamically tailored to the user’s market, not just filtered by it.

Right now the platform needs to move from being a strong life sciences-focused intelligence product to being a universal recruitment intelligence platform that adapts its language, signal logic, modules, and outputs depending on the user’s chosen industry, market structure, and commercial model.

The system must not feel like a life sciences tool with generic filters layered on top. It must feel like the product understands the recruiter’s market and reshapes itself accordingly across the entire app.

1. Core product behaviour

Everything in the app must be driven by the user’s configured market settings.

This includes:

industry

subsector

niche / specialty

geography

company type

company stage

commercial model

signal preferences

The product should use those settings to shape:

dashboard content

market radar

hiring signals

companies

watchlist

venture / investor intelligence

market intelligence

candidate matcher

BD scripts

content studio

map / geographic intelligence

terminology and labels where needed

This means the platform is not only filtering results. It is also changing which signals matter, how modules are framed, what language is used, and what entities are shown.

2. Industry-specific signal logic across the app

Different industries care about different signals.

The platform must dynamically adjust the signal types shown and prioritised depending on the market the recruiter works in.

Example: Life Sciences

Relevant signals may include:

funding

hiring

leadership movement

partnerships

clinical milestones

regulatory milestones

pipeline progression

licensing / collaboration deals

new company launches

investor activity

Example: Legal

Relevant signals may include:

partner moves

law firm office launches

mergers between firms

major mandates / case wins

team buildouts

lateral hiring

practice group expansion

revenue growth

new client wins

Example: Construction / Built Environment

Relevant signals may include:

contract wins

project launches

geographic expansion

major hires

new site openings

M&A

revenue growth

public sector awards

framework wins

Example: Technology

Relevant signals may include:

funding

hiring

product launches

executive changes

new market entries

revenue milestones

PE investment

M&A

customer growth

partnerships

The Market Radar and all other relevant modules must reflect the signal model that makes sense for that industry.

Do not hard-code life sciences logic into a universal product.

3. Commercial model awareness: funding vs revenue vs ownership structure

The platform must support different business realities depending on the market.

Some industries are highly funding-driven. Others are revenue-driven. Some are VC-backed. Some are PE-backed. Some are neither.

The product must recognise this and reflect it properly.

Funding-driven markets

Example:

biotech

venture-backed SaaS

deep tech startups

Relevant indicators:

funding rounds

investor syndicates

company stage

capital raised

runway implications

expected leadership buildout

Revenue-driven markets

Example:

legal

construction

many services businesses

mature industrial firms

Relevant indicators:

revenue growth

contract wins

office expansion

major new clients

market share growth

team growth

PE-backed markets

Example:

professional services

industrials

healthcare services

B2B SaaS roll-ups

Relevant indicators:

PE ownership

platform / add-on acquisitions

leadership changes post-investment

growth mandates

scale-up hiring

transformation or integration signals

The platform must allow the underlying commercial logic to shift depending on the market profile so it feels natively relevant.

4. Venture / investor tab must evolve into broader capital / ownership intelligence

The current Venture Intelligence tab cannot remain venture-only if the app is to work across markets.

It needs to support both:

Venture Capital

Private Equity

And potentially broader ownership / capital intelligence over time.

Rename and/or structure this section so it can support:

venture firms

PE firms

portfolio companies

platform companies

investment activity

ownership-related movement

If the user’s market is VC-heavy, the module should feel venture-oriented.

If the user’s market is PE-heavy, it should reflect PE ownership, platforms, add-ons, portfolio growth, and leadership changes.

If the user’s market does not rely heavily on investors at all, this module should either:

adapt to broader ownership / company backing intelligence
or

reduce emphasis in the navigation / dashboard

This part of the product must be context-aware.

5. Dynamic labels and terminology

The app should use terminology that fits the user’s market where appropriate.

Examples:

“Venture Intelligence” may not make sense in PE-heavy or non-investor-led markets

“Pipeline milestone” is relevant in biotech but not in legal

“Practice expansion” may matter in law

“Contract award” may matter in construction

“Platform acquisition” may matter in PE-backed sectors

The product should be able to adapt section language, signal labels, and detail descriptions depending on the market profile.

This should not feel jarring or overdone, but it should be contextually smart.

6. Settings must drive every module consistently

Once the user defines their market in Settings / Market Builder, that logic must cascade across the site.

Examples:

If user selects:

Life Sciences → Oncology Biotech → US → VC-backed → C-suite

The system should prioritise:

funding

pipeline / clinical milestones

leadership movement

new portcos

likely CSO / CMO / COO hires

If user selects:

Legal → Corporate Law → UK → Revenue-driven → Partner / Senior Associate hiring

The system should prioritise:

partner moves

practice launches

office openings

new mandates

revenue growth

expansion signals

If user selects:

Construction → Commercial Contractors → Middle East → Project-led / Revenue-led

The system should prioritise:

project awards

contract wins

regional expansion

major site openings

senior commercial or project leadership hires

Every major module must reflect this logic.

The system should feel like:
this platform is built for my market
not
this platform is a biotech tool pretending to be generic

7. Specific module behaviour updates
A. Dashboard / Command Centre

The dashboard should surface the most relevant signal types and summaries for the user’s market.

The overview cards, top signals, and recommended actions should all be based on the configured industry and commercial model.

B. Market Radar

This must become a truly dynamic signal feed.
It should only show and prioritise the signal categories that matter to the market the recruiter has defined.

C. Hiring Signals

The ranking logic should reflect the types of events that usually precede hiring in that market.

For some markets that may be funding.
For others, it may be revenue growth, contract wins, M&A, or office expansion.

D. Companies

The way companies are scored and prioritised should reflect the market logic.
A company in a revenue-led sector should not be ranked using venture-style assumptions.

E. Capital / Ownership Intelligence

This must show VC or PE appropriately depending on the market.

F. Market Intelligence

The macro trends, growth areas, and demand forecasts should reflect the specific market.

G. Watchlist

Movement on watched accounts must reflect relevant signal types for the user’s market.

H. Map / geographic intelligence

What is plotted geographically should also reflect the correct signal categories for the market.

8. Candidate Matcher / CV Parser update

The Candidate Matcher and CV Parser need to be cleaned up and made more flexible and controllable.

The overall layout should be:

Parser / upload and notes on the left

Parsed profile building live on the right

Below that: company target list and e-shot builder

The whole page must feel clean, structured, and seamless.

9. Candidate Matcher workflow and layout
Left side: Parser input panel

Allow the user to:

upload a PDF CV

upload a Word document CV / resume

add interview notes or recruiter notes

type additional information into a text box

This side should be the input / context area.

Right side: Live profile builder

As the CV and notes are parsed, build the candidate profile on the right-hand side.

This profile should be structured and editable.

It should include:

company history

job titles

specialty

USP

achievements

recruiter notes / additional context

any market-specific fit factors

The profile should be clear and commercially useful, not a blob of AI text.

10. CV parsing logic and output structure

The parsed profile should start with context:

companies worked for

job titles held

current / most recent role

relevant career progression

Then derive:

specialty

market focus

leadership level

USP

key achievements

likely target company fit

This needs to feel accurate and recruiter-grade because it will feed directly into the e-shot builder.

11. Company list below parsed profile

Below the profile, show a target company list.

This list should be clean and simple.

Initially show:

company names only or very minimal company rows

Then when the user clicks a company, open a detailed fit view.

The list should be ranked by:

fit to candidate

fit to recruiter’s market

likely need

relevant signal alignment

12. Company fit detail and e-shot builder

When a company is selected, show:

company name

why the company is a fit

relevant company context

likely role alignment

relevant signal or growth context

e-shot builder

This should feel like the action panel.

13. Add control over e-shot generation

The CV Parser / Candidate Matcher should work much more like the Content Studio in terms of control and flexibility.

The recruiter should be able to shape the output rather than just accept one rigid draft.

Add controls for:

Tone

Examples:

formal

conversational

commercial

consultative

direct

warm

high-level executive

Structure

Examples:

concise

standard executive search

candidate-led

signal-led

relationship-led

bullet-heavy

narrative-led

Personalisation

Allow fields or settings for:

greeting style

company context

candidate highlight emphasis

achievement emphasis

soft close vs stronger CTA

This should make the user feel they have control over how the e-shot is built.

14. End-to-end Candidate Matcher flow

The final flow should be:

Upload CV / resume

Add notes / context

Review parsed profile on the right

Edit / refine profile if needed

Review ranked target companies below

Click company

See why the fit makes sense

Build tailored e-shot with tone / structure controls

This needs to feel smooth, premium, and connected.

Do not make it feel like separate tools stitched together.

15. Final objective

The whole app should become a market-adaptive recruitment intelligence platform where:

each industry sees the signals that matter to them

each commercial model is represented properly

every module reflects the user’s market settings

the Venture tab evolves into broader capital / ownership intelligence where needed

the Candidate Matcher becomes a polished candidate-to-market workflow

the e-shot builder gives the user meaningful control over output tone and structure

The result should be a platform that feels:

highly personalised

commercially intelligent

clean and premium

relevant across many recruitment sectors

seamless in flow from intelligence to action
